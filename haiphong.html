Hải Phòng - Hai Phong

Hai Phong is the capital is Vietnam's third-largest city, ranking behind Ho Chi Minh City and Hanoi,
covering an area of 1,526.52 km², has a population of 2,310,280 as of 2023.

1. Hai Phong Attractions

1.1 Historical Places

Du Hang Pagoda

Du Hang Pagoda is an ancient pagoda, dating back over a thousand years, is an important Buddhist site in Hai Phong. It features traditional Vietnamese architecture and serene surroundings for spiritual visitors.

Du Hang Pagoda - @chuaviettoancau.com

Hang Kenh Communal House

Built during the 17th century, this communal house is dedicated to King Ngo Quyen and showcases intricate wood carvings, reflecting the region’s artistic heritage.

Hang Kenh Communal House - Getty Images

Bach Dang River Historical Site

The river played a key role in Vietnam's victory over foreign invaders during naval battles. Memorials and monuments mark the site’s historical significance.

Bach Dang River Historical Site - diendandoanhnghiep.vn

1.2 Scenic Area

As a port city, Hai Phong is also renowned for its beaches and many impressive tourist attractions.

Do Son Beach

Do Son Beach - vina.com

A popular seaside getaway, Do Son Beach features long sandy shores, lush hills, and a relaxed atmosphere, making it ideal for leisure and recreation.

Cat Ba Island

Known for its stunning landscapes, pristine beaches, and lush forests, Cat Ba Island is a must-visit destination. It is also part of the UNESCO-listed Cat Ba Archipelago Biosphere Reserve.

Cat Ba Island - lonelyplanet

Lan Ha Bay

Located near Ha Long Bay, Lan Ha Bay is famous for its emerald-green waters, limestone karsts, and secluded beaches, offering a more tranquil experience compared to its neighboring bay.

Lan Ha Bay - Halong hub

2. Food Travel in Hai Phong

Hai Phong also boasts a wide variety of enticing local specialties.

Banh Da Cua - Crab Noodle Soup

Banh Da Cua - Getty Images

A signature dish of Hai Phong, this noodle soup features a rich crab broth, flat rice noodles, fried tofu, morning glory, and sometimes seafood toppings like shrimp or fish cakes.

Nem Cua Be (Sea Crab Spring Rolls)

Nem Cua Be - vitot food

These square-shaped spring rolls are filled with fresh crab meat, minced pork, mushrooms, and vermicelli, wrapped in rice paper, and fried to golden perfection.

Banh Duc Tau (Tapioca Flour Cake with Toppings):

Banh Duc Tau - Getty Images

A unique Hai Phong snack made from steamed tapioca flour, topped with tofu, shrimp, and a tangy, spicy vinegar sauce.

3. Hai Phong's culture and festival

Hai Phong's culture is deeply rooted in its coastal heritage, blending traditional Vietnamese customs with the vibrant spirit of a bustling port city.

Red Flamboyant Flower Festival - Le hoi Hoa Phuong Do:

Red Flamboyant Flower Festival - Pinterest

Held annually in May, this festival celebrates the city's symbol, the flamboyant tree. It features parades, art performances, and cultural activities showcasing Hai Phong's identity.

Do Son Buffalo Fighting Festival - Lễ hội Chọi Trâu Đồ Sơn

Do Son Buffalo Fighting Festival - Getty Images

A unique and historic festival held in the Do Son District, where water buffaloes compete in ceremonial battles. This event is steeped in spiritual significance and attracts large crowds.

Traditional Boat Racing

Traditional Boat Racing - Internet

Often organized during Tet or other local celebrations, this event reflects Hai Phong's maritime culture and fosters community spirit.
