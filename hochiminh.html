Thành phố Hồ Chí Minh - Ho Chi Minh city

Ho Chi Minh City, also known as Saigon, is the largest and most dynamic city in Vietnam.
The city spans an area of approximately 2,095 square kilometers and has a population of over 9 million people,
it is the economic, cultural, and commercial hub of the country.

1. Ho Chi Minh city Attractions

1.1 Historical Places

War Remnants Museum

A poignant museum documenting the brutal history of the Vietnam War through exhibits of photographs, military equipment, and personal accounts.

War Remnants Museum - Getty Images

Reunification Palace

A historical landmark where the fall of Saigon occurred in 1975, marking the end of the Vietnam War. The palace showcases the opulent lifestyle of the former South Vietnamese government.

Reunification Palace - Getty Images

Notre-Dame Cathedral Basilica of Saigon

Built during French colonial rule, this iconic cathedral stands as a symbol of the city's European influence and is a beautiful example of neo-Romanesque architecture.

Notre-Dame Cathedral Basilica of Saigon - Getty Images

Saigon Central Post Office

Travel to the Hoàng Thành Thăng Long Imperial Citadel, a UNESCO World Heritage site, to discover Vietnam’s historical significance. Explore ancient structures, learn about the nation’s royal past, and enjoy the cultural heritage of this iconic landmark.

Saigon Central Post Office - Getty Images

1.2 Scenic Area

Ben Thanh Market

Ben Thanh Market - Pinterest

One of the oldest and most famous markets in the city, offering everything from fresh produce to handmade goods, surrounded by vibrant street life.

Saigon River

The river is a vital part of the city’s landscape, with boat cruises offering beautiful views of Ho Chi Minh City's skyline and iconic bridges.

Saigon River - Getty Images

Can Gio Mangrove Forest

A UNESCO biosphere reserve, this coastal area offers serene nature trails, wildlife watching, and mangrove forest exploration.

Can Gio Mangrove Forest - Getty Images

2. Food Travel in Ho Chi Minh

Banh Mi

Pho - Getty Images

A French-inspired sandwich, typically filled with a mix of meats, pate, fresh herbs, and crunchy vegetables, all in a soft baguette.

Com Tam - Broken Rice

Com Tam - Getty Images

Com Tam is a popular Vietnamese dish made with broken rice, often served with grilled pork, pickled vegetables, and fish sauce. It's a flavorful and comforting meal enjoyed across Vietnam.

Banh Xeo - Vietnamese Pancakes

Banh Xeo - Pinterest

Banh Xeo is a Vietnamese savory pancake made with rice flour, turmeric, and filled with shrimp, pork, and bean sprouts. It's crispy, delicious, and often served with fresh herbs and dipping sauce.

3. Ho Chi Minh culture and festival

Tet Nguyen Dan - Lunar New Year

Lunar New Year - Best Price Travel

The most important and widely celebrated festival in Vietnam, Tết marks the arrival of the new lunar year with family reunions, street festivities, dragon dances, and vibrant flower displays.

Saigon Notre-Dame Cathedral Festival

Saigon Notre-Dame Cathedral Festival - Getty Images

The Saigon Notre-Dame Cathedral Festival celebrates the iconic cathedral in Ho Chi Minh City, featuring religious events, cultural activities, and vibrant performances. It draws both locals and tourists to honor the history and architecture of this landmark.
