Đà Nẵng - Da Nang

Da Nang spans an area of 1,284.9 km² and has a population of over 1.1 million (2023)
Da Nang serves as a gateway to several UNESCO World Heritage sites, including Hoi An, My Son Sanctuary, and Hue.

1. Da Nang Attractions

Da Nang attracts visitors from around the world.

1.1 Historical Places

Hai Van Pass (Deo Hai Van):

A historic mountain pass that served as a strategic defense point, offering stunning views and remnants of ancient military structures.

Hai Van Pass - Pinterest

Ngu Hanh Son - Marble Mountains

A cluster of limestone and marble hills with ancient caves, pagodas, and historical significance tied to Buddhist and Cham cultures.

Marble Mountains - Getty Images

Thanh Dien Hai Citadel

A 19th-century fortress that played a critical role in resisting French colonial forces.

Thanh Dien Hai Citadel - Getty Images

1.2 Scenic Area

My Khe Beach

My Khe Beach - Pinterest

One of Vietnam’s most famous beaches, known for its white sands and clear waters, ideal for relaxation and water sports.

Ba Na Hills

A mountain resort featuring the Golden Bridge, European-inspired architecture, and beautiful gardens.

Ba Na Hills - Getty Images

Dragon Bridge

An architectural marvel that lights up at night, symbolizing Da Nang’s modernity and vibrance.

Dragon Bridge - Getty Images

Golden Bridge

It is famous for its unique design, where a pair of giant, weathered hands seem to support the bridge, creating a breathtaking visual effect. The bridge spans 150 meters and offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and lush landscapes.

Golden Bridge - Getty Images

2. Food Travel in Da Nang

Seafood

Seafood - Getty Images

Nem Lui - Getty Images

3. Da Nang culture and festival

Da Nang's culture is a vibrant mix of traditional Vietnamese heritage and modern influences, shaped by its coastal location and historical significance.

Da Nang International Fireworks Festival (DIFF)

Da Nang International Fireworks Festival (DIFF) - Getty Images

A world-class fireworks competition held annually, attracting participants and spectators globally.

Quan The Am Festival - Lễ hội Quán Thế Âm

Quan The Am Festival - Getty Images

A Buddhist festival held at Marble Mountains, featuring spiritual ceremonies and traditional performances.
