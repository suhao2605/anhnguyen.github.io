Thừa Thiên-Huế - Hue

Thua Thien Hue is famous for its royal history, imperial architecture, and vibrant traditions.

1. Thua Thien Hue Attractions

1.1 Historical Places

Hanoi, with its 1000 years of culture, is home to many historical sites.

Imperial City - Kinh Thanh Hue

A UNESCO World Heritage site, the Imperial City is a massive complex of palaces, temples, and gates. It was the political and cultural center of Vietnam during the Nguyen Dynasty (1802–1945).

Imperial City - Pinterest

Thien Mu Pagoda

One of Vietnam’s oldest and most iconic pagodas, located on the banks of the Perfume River, it is an important cultural and religious landmark.

Thien Mu Pagoda - Getty Images

Tomb of Emperor Khai Dinh

The Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum is the the resting place of Vietnamese revolutionary leader and President Ho Chi Minh. Located in Ba Dinh Square, this is one of Hanoi’s most significant historical sites. The mausoleum is where major national events take place and is a must-see for anyone interested in learning more about the life and legacy of President Ho Chi Minh.

Tomb of Emperor Khai Dinh - Getty Images

Hue Royal Antiquities Museum

Travel to the Hoàng Thành Thăng Long Imperial Citadel, a UNESCO World Heritage site, to discover Vietnam’s historical significance. Explore ancient structures, learn about the nation’s royal past, and enjoy the cultural heritage of this iconic landmark.

Hue Royal Antiquities Museum - Getty Images

1.2 Scenic Area

Huong River

Huong River - Pinterest

A picturesque river that flows through Hue, offering scenic boat trips with views of historic landmarks, pagodas, and lush landscapes.

Thuan An Beach

The Hanoi Opera House is an impressive and majestic architectural masterpiece, ranked as a National Heritage Site. It is associated with many significant events in the capital. The Hanoi Opera House is one of the most prominent theaters in the country, featuring unique architecture and renowned for hosting numerous classical art performances.

Thuan An Beach - Getty Images

2. Food Travel in Hue

Bun Bo Hue (Hue Beef Noodle Soup)

Bun Bo Hue - Getty Images

A signature dish of Hue, this spicy noodle soup is made with beef, pork, and herbs, offering a flavorful, rich broth.

Cơm Hến - Clam Rice

Clam Rice - Getty Images

A traditional Hue dish made with rice, clams, and a mix of herbs, vegetables, and crunchy pork cracklings.

Banh Beo - Steamed Rice Cakes

Banh Beo - Eva.vn

These small, steamed rice cakes are topped with shrimp, pork, and crispy onions, often served with a side of fish sauce.

Banh Khoai (Hue-style Pancake)

Banh Khoai - Nang Coffee

A savory pancake filled with shrimp, pork, and vegetables, served with lettuce and herbs for wrapping.

3. Hanoi culture and festival

Thua Thien Hue is deeply rooted in Vietnam’s imperial past and is known for preserving its ancient customs, traditions, and local crafts, including intricate wood carvings, royal music (Nhã nhạc), and colorful festivals.

Hue Festival

Hue Festival - Pinterest

Held every two years, the Hue Festival is the most famous cultural event in the region, featuring traditional music, royal processions, art performances, and international cultural exchanges.

Hung Kings Temple Festival

Hung Kings Temple Festival - Pinterest

The Dong Da Festival, also known as the Gò Dong Da Festival, is held to commemorate the historic victory of the Ngọc Hồi - Dong Da battle, led by King Quang Trung - Nguyễn Huệ. This is one of Hanoi's major festivals, which has been celebrated for over 200 years and is held on a city-wide scale. The festival takes place annually during the Lunar New Year at Quang Trung Ward, Dong Da

Royal Games Festival

Royal Games Festival - Internet

Held during the Hue Festival, this festival recreates the games and activities once played at the imperial court, showcasing Hue’s royal heritage.
