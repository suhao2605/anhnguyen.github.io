Hà Nội - Hanoi

Hanoi is the capital and second-most populous city of Vietnam.
The city encompasses an area of 3,359.84 km2 and as of 2023, a population of 8,5 million.
Hanoi consists of 12 urban districts, 17 rural districts, and one district-level town

1. Hanoi Attractions

Hanoi has many prominent tourist attractions.

1.1 Historical Places

Hanoi, with its 1000 years of culture, is home to many historical sites.

Hoan Kiem Lake

Hoan Kiem Lake, also known as Sword Lake, is one of the most famous landmarks and symbols of Hanoi. It is not only beautiful in terms of scenery but also rich in historical stories. Visitors can visit the Ngoc Son Temple on a small island in the middle of the lake or take a walk around the lake to enjoy the peaceful atmosphere.

Hoan Kiem Lake - Getty Images

The Temple of Literature

The Temple of Literature is a famous historical site built in 1070 under the Ly Dynasty, and it is dedicated to Confucius. It is one of the oldest architectural works in Hanoi and a symbol of Vietnam's educational system. The temple is known for its large gates, stone steles of doctoral laureates, and a serene, ancient architectural space.

The Temple of Literature - Getty Images

Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum

The Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum is the the resting place of Vietnamese revolutionary leader and President Ho Chi Minh. Located in Ba Dinh Square, this is one of Hanoi’s most significant historical sites. The mausoleum is where major national events take place and is a must-see for anyone interested in learning more about the life and legacy of President Ho Chi Minh.

Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum- Getty Images

Hoàng Thành Thăng Long - Thang Long Imperial Citadel

Travel to the Hoàng Thành Thăng Long Imperial Citadel, a UNESCO World Heritage site, to discover Vietnam’s historical significance. Explore ancient structures, learn about the nation’s royal past, and enjoy the cultural heritage of this iconic landmark.

Hoàng Thành Thăng Long - Thang Long Imperial Citadel - Getty Images

1.2 Scenic Area

Hanoi has many beautiful sightseeing locations and amazing landmarks.

Dong Xuan Market

Dong Xuan Market - Pinterest

This is a wholesale market selling a variety of goods. When visiting the market, tourists can freely shop at the stalls (clothes, shoes, electronics, fabrics, children’s items, souvenirs, candies, etc.). The market is very large, so be sure to visit each floor to experience all the interesting aspects of the market.

Hanoi Opera House

The Hanoi Opera House is an impressive and majestic architectural masterpiece, ranked as a National Heritage Site. It is associated with many significant events in the capital. The Hanoi Opera House is one of the most prominent theaters in the country, featuring unique architecture and renowned for hosting numerous classical art performances.

2. Food Travel in Hanoi

Hanoi has many famous dishes that are well-known to international friends.

Pho

Pho - Getty Images

Pho is a traditional Vietnamese noodle soup made from broth, rice noodles, herbs, and meat, usually beef or chicken. It is one of the most famous dishes in Hanoi, known for its delicate and rich flavor. The soup is typically served for breakfast and enjoyed by locals and tourists alike.

Banh Cuon

Banh Cuon - Getty Images

Banh Cuon is a traditional Hanoi dish consisting of rice rolls filled with minced pork, mushrooms, and fried shallots, served with a side of fish sauce and herbs. It has a soft texture and a subtle flavor, making it a popular choice among locals and visitors.

Banh Goi (Hanoi Dumplings)

Banh Goi (Hanoi Dumplings) - Eva.vn

Banh Goi, also known as Hanoi dumplings, are crispy pastries filled with minced pork, mushrooms, and other savory ingredients. They are deep-fried until golden and served with a dipping sauce, making them a popular street food snack in Hanoi.

Egg Coffee

Egg Coffee - Nang Coffee

Egg Coffee is a unique Hanoi drink made from egg yolks, sugar, condensed milk, and coffee. The result is a creamy, rich, and sweet coffee that is a beloved specialty of Hanoi. It's a perfect drink for those who want to try something different from traditional coffee.

3. Hanoi culture and festival

Hanoi is home to a rich array of festivals and traditions that are deeply intertwined with its history and cultural heritage.

Co Loa Temple Festival

Co Loa Temple Festival - Best Price Travel

The Co Loa Festival is held to honor An Dương Vương Thục Phán, the legendary hero of ancient Vietnam. In addition to paying tribute to this great king, the festival also reminds the people of the noble tradition of “Be grateful for what you have” a long-standing cultural value of the Vietnamese people.

Dong Da Festival

Dong Da Festival - Getty Images

The Dong Da Festival, also known as the Gò Dong Da Festival, is held to commemorate the historic victory of the Ngọc Hồi - Dong Da battle, led by King Quang Trung - Nguyễn Huệ. This is one of Hanoi's major festivals, which has been celebrated for over 200 years and is held on a city-wide scale. The festival takes place annually during the Lunar New Year at Quang Trung Ward, Dong Da

Hanoi Ao Dai Tourism Festival

Hanoi Ao Dai Tourism Festival - Internet

The Hanoi Ao Dai Tourism Festival is an opportunity to honor the beauty of the traditional Vietnamese Ao Dai and promote tourism to visitors from all over. The 2023 festival was inaugurated at the stage of Bà Kiệu Temple Garden, featuring over 50 booths showcasing Ao Dai products, accessories, and traditional items that left a lasting impression on attendees.
